Antigens Present: Blood group AB has both A and B antigens on the surface of its red blood cells. Antibodies in Plasma: Individuals with blood group AB have no anti-A or anti-B antibodies in their plasma. This means they can receive blood from donors with A, B, AB, or O blood types without agglutination (clumping of red blood cells).
Universal Recipient: Blood group AB is often referred to as the "universal recipient" because individuals with this blood type can receive blood from any of the four main blood types (A, B, AB, and O). This flexibility makes it easier to find compatible blood for transfusions.
